Product Design

Geneva Drive Can Dispenser

The goal of the project was to design and manufacture a simple machine with a limited budget. The frame was laser cut and the drive was machined with EDM. 3D-printing was utilized to accurately fit the drive in the frame. My responsibilities in the project group were preliminary design, material selection and design and assembly of the ramp part of the frame. The assembled dispenser functioned well, despite the thickness limitations of the laser cutter that caused accuracy issues as the parts had to be cut twice.

3D Printed Gear Bearing

As a part of an Advanced Manufacturing course, the objective was to model and 3D print an object with moving parts, conformal cooling, lattice structure and topological optimization. I decided to do my take on the gear bearing, inspired by the design of Emmett Lalish at Thingiverse. My version has straight gears with slopes at the end instead of herringbone gears. Additionally, a conformal cooling pipe was added and the casing was topologically optimized using Creo Simulate FEM analysis and CAESS ProTOpCI. Self-Organizing Map Study

The task was to research how the Self-Organizing Map (SOM) works and then apply it to real-world data with Matlab. The 9-dimensional data set consisted of nutritional values of 961 food items, so the objective was to find dependencies between the food properties and then cluster the data and analyze the clusters by generating a U-matrix, component plane and PCA-projection visualizations. A total of six food categories were recognized.

Game Prototyping

Exploring Movement

The idea of the game is to focus everything around movement abilities of the player. The game starts with very basic, limited set of movement options, but expands greatly as the player explores the world and can reach new areas as new movement options are unlocked. With skilful control, it's possible to reach areas without finding all the available movement enhancements. The game engine is Unity and scripting is done in C#. Credit for the character controller template goes to Erik Roystan Ross.

Hex Grid Area Control Strategy

The goal of the game is to gather resources, build structures, spread and control the map more than the opponent. Unlike a usual real time strategy game, there are no separate controllable units but one single mass of liquid that spreads according to simple rules. The game is done in Unity scripted in C#. Credit for the hex grid the game is based on goes to Jasper Flick from Catlike Coding.

Procedural Tactics

This is a subject that I've tried tackling before, but wasn't able to before learning Unity. Previously, game engine and code extensibility limitations were major road blocks for this type of project, but now I've managed to create a working prototype of a space survival tactics game with simple procedural generation and other roguelike elements.

Design Museum: Design Club Open Call

The goal of the design competition was to create an installation that applies sound and visuals to create a corporeal experience. Our team's exhibition contains an interactive puzzle which utilizes operable projectors that combine their projections to reveal hidden pieces of a code. I was responsible for the game design and user experience aspects. The visual arts are done by Eetu Lehmusvaara and the production and sound design are done by Karoliina Kauhanen. The plan has been submitted to the ongoing competition and this page will be updated when the results are in!

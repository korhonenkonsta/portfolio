Overview

Product Design

Geneva Drive Can Dispenser

The goal of the project was to design and manufacture a simple machine with a limited budget. The frame was laser cut and the drive was machined with EDM. 3D-printing was utilized to accurately fit the drive in the frame. My responsibilities in the project group were preliminary design, material selection and design and assembly of the ramp part of the frame. The assembled dispenser functioned well, despite the thickness limitations of the laser cutter that caused accuracy issues as the parts had to be cut twice.

3D Printed Gear Bearing

As a part of an Advanced Manufacturing course, the objective was to model and 3D print an object with moving parts, conformal cooling, lattice structure and topological optimization. I decided to do my take on the gear bearing, inspired by the design of Emmett Lalish at Thingiverse. My version has straight gears with slopes at the end instead of herringbone gears. Additionally, a conformal cooling pipe was added and the casing was topologically optimized using Creo Simulate FEM analysis and CAESS ProTOpCI. Hex Area Control Strategy

The goal of the game is to gather resources, build structures, spread and control the map more than the opponent. Unlike a usual real time strategy game, there are no separate controllable units but one single mass of liquid that spreads according to simple rules. Credit for the hex grid the game is based on goes to Jasper Flick from Catlike Coding.

Procedural Tactics

This is a subject that I've tried tackling before, but wasn't able to before learning Unity. Previously, game engine and code extensibility limitations were major road blocks for this type of project, but now I've managed to create a working prototype of a space survival tactics game with simple procedural generation and other roguelike elements.
